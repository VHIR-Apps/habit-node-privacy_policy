By downloading, installing, or using HabitNode, you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the App immediately.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Data Stored Locally on Your Device

The following data is stored exclusively on your device using the Hive encrypted local database and is never transmitted to our servers unless you explicitly enable a cloud feature:

  • Profile Information: Display name, avatar emoji, tagline, bio, country code (all optional)
  • Habit & Routine Data: Habit names, descriptions, categories, schedules, goals, priorities, notes, completion records, study targets, and progress logs
  • Study Sessions: Pomodoro timer data, study routine schedules, session history, subject targets, and daily study time records
  • Personal Notes: Note titles, content, tags, and timestamps
  • Missed Habit Reflections: Optional reasons provided when skipping a habit — used locally for smart reminders only
  • Achievement Data: Badges earned, XP points, level progress, streaks, and trophy cabinet
  • App Preferences: Theme mode, notification settings, sound settings, alarm configurations, and privacy toggles
  • Study Analytics: Daily, weekly, and all-time study minutes — stored locally for statistics display
  • Blocked Users List: UIDs of users you have blocked — stored locally only, never sent to any server
  • Liked Profiles Cache: Local reference to profiles you have liked — used to display your "You Liked" tab without repeated network calls

1.2 Data Stored in the Cloud (Optional & User-Initiated)

📊 Global Leaderboard & Public Profile (Opt-In Required) v4.1 Updated

If you voluntarily join the global leaderboard, the following data is stored in Google Cloud Firestore:

  • Display name, avatar emoji, tagline, bio, country code (all editable by you)
  • Unique UID — your Firebase Authentication UID
  • XP score, level, study hours, badges unlocked count
  • Daily score, weekly score
  • Posts you share on the Social Feed
  • Privacy settings: showLevel, showBadges, showStudyHours toggles
  • Pro status (boolean — not payment details)
  • Last active timestamp
  • Like count: Total number of likes received on your profile (integer only — not individual liker identities in the main document)

Your Privacy Controls: You may toggle visibility of your level, badges, and study hours independently. You may opt out or permanently delete your cloud profile at any time via Settings → Leaderboard → Opt Out or Delete Profile.

⚠️ What other users can see: Only your display name, avatar, tagline, bio, country code, XP, level (if enabled), badges (if enabled), study time (if enabled), social posts, and total like count. Your email address is never shown to other users.

❤️ Profile Like System (Opt-In — Leaderboard Required) v4.1 New

HabitNode includes a Profile Like feature that allows users to show appreciation for other users' public profiles. When you use this feature:

  • What is stored in Firestore: When you like a profile, your UID and a server-generated timestamp are stored in a likes subcollection under that user's Firestore document
  • What the profile owner sees: Total like count on their profile, and a list of users who liked them (showing display name and avatar, fetched from their public profile)
  • What other users see: Only the total like count — not who liked the profile
  • "You Liked" Tab: You can view all profiles you have liked in the Leaderboard → "You Liked" tab. This list is fetched from Firestore and cached locally for performance
  • Unlike: You may remove your like at any time by tapping the like button again — your UID and timestamp are immediately and permanently deleted from Firestore
  • Self-like prevention: You cannot like your own profile — enforced both in-app and at the Firestore rules level

Data stored per like record in Firestore:

  • likerUid — your Firebase UID
  • likedAt — server timestamp of when the like was created

Deletion: If you delete your cloud profile, all like records associated with your UID as the liker are removed. If the liked profile is deleted, the entire likes subcollection is removed automatically.

🔒 Like data is never shared with advertisers, third parties, or used for profiling purposes. It exists solely to enable the social appreciation feature within HabitNode.

☁️ Google Drive Auto-Backup (User-Initiated) v4.1 Updated

When you enable Google Drive backup, your data is encrypted and uploaded to your private Google Drive appDataFolder. This folder is invisible to other apps and accessible only by you and HabitNode.

Schema v2 — Data covered by backup:

  • All habits, routines, daily study routines
  • All study sessions and study targets
  • Leaderboard profile (local copy)
  • All personal notes
  • App settings and preferences
  • Notification history

Auto-Restore on Sign-In: When you sign in with Google on a new device or after reinstalling, HabitNode automatically checks for an existing Google Drive backup and restores your data silently in the background. This restore is triggered only when no local data exists on the device.

Backup Frequency Options: Every change (with debounce), daily, weekly, or on app exit. Works on both WiFi and mobile data. An offline pending queue ensures no data loss when connectivity is unavailable.

Chat messages and profile likes are NOT included in Google Drive backup — they are stored in Firestore and managed separately.

💬 Social Chat & Direct Messaging (Optional — Account Required) v4.1 Updated

HabitNode includes a built-in real-time direct messaging system. When you use this feature:

  • Messages are transmitted over HTTPS/TLS encrypted channels
  • Messages are stored in Google Cloud Firestore under your conversation ID
  • Each message contains: sender UID, message text, timestamp, and isChallenge flag
  • Free users have a daily message limit (20 messages/day) tracked in Firestore
  • Pro users have unlimited messaging

Your controls: Delete individual messages (Unsend) · Clear entire chat history · Block users · Report users · The blocked users list is stored locally on your device only

🔒 Your private conversations are never shared with advertisers, third parties, or other users outside the conversation.

🔔 Chat Notifications (Local Only) v4.1

HabitNode uses a Firestore real-time listener to detect new incoming chat messages while the app is in the foreground. When a new message arrives:

  • A local notification is displayed on your device using flutter_local_notifications
  • The sender's display name and avatar are fetched from Firestore to personalize the notification
  • No data is sent to external push notification servers for chat notifications
  • Notifications are not shown when you have that specific chat conversation open
  • Chat notifications do not auto-navigate to the inbox — a snackbar with an "Open" action is shown instead, respecting user intent

This system works only while the app is running. It does not use FCM push notifications for chat messages.

📢 Social Feed & Posts (Optional)

If you choose to share a post on the Social Feed (maximum 500 words per post), the following data is stored publicly in Firestore:

  • Your author UID, display name, avatar emoji
  • Your current XP score at time of posting
  • Post text content, server timestamp, and client-generated timestamp (for offline sort)
  • Like count, dislike count, and report count (integers only)

1.3 Connectivity-Based Behavior

📵 Offline-First Architecture v4.1 New

HabitNode is designed to work fully offline. When your device has no internet connection:

  • All habit tracking, study timer, notes, alarms, and local analytics work without any network connection
  • No error pages or crash screens are shown due to connectivity issues
  • Cloud features (leaderboard, chat, backup, likes) are gracefully disabled — no data loss occurs
  • Cached leaderboard data and profiles are displayed from local Firestore cache when available
  • When connectivity is restored, the app automatically resumes cloud operations in the background

The ConnectivityService monitors network status continuously. Online-dependent tasks (admin messages, leaderboard sync, backup) are queued and executed only when a valid internet connection is confirmed.

1.4 Account Information (Google Sign-In)

When you sign in with Google, we receive your Google email address, display name, profile photo URL, and unique Firebase Authentication UID. This data is used solely for authentication and cloud feature access. It is never sold or shared with advertisers.

Auto-Restore on First Sign-In: When you sign in for the first time on a device (including after reinstallation), HabitNode automatically checks Google Drive for an existing backup and restores your data if found and no local data exists. This provides seamless data portability across devices.

1.5 Unique User Identifier (UID) System

🆔 About Your UID

Every HabitNode user who signs in receives a unique identifier (UID) generated by Firebase Authentication. This UID:

  • Is visible on your own profile and can be copied and shared by you
  • Is used by other users to search for you and send direct messages
  • Is stored as part of your leaderboard profile in Firestore
  • Is stored in the likes subcollection when you like other profiles
  • Is not your email address and does not directly reveal personal information
  • Cannot be changed — it is permanently linked to your Google account

3. Data Storage & Security

Local Storage

All on-device data is stored in a Hive encrypted local database. Encryption keys are stored securely on your device and are never transmitted. This includes habits, notes, sessions, settings, the local copy of your leaderboard profile, and your blocked users list.

Cloud Storage

Leaderboard profiles, chat messages, social posts, and profile like records are stored in Google Cloud Firestore with server-side Security Rules that ensure users can only read/write their own data. Like records are stored in subcollections accessible only by the profile owner and the liker. Google Drive backups are encrypted before upload and stored in your private appDataFolder.

Security Measures

  • HTTPS / TLS encryption for all data in transit
  • Hive AES encryption for all data at rest on-device
  • Firebase OAuth 2.0 token-based authentication with automatic token refresh
  • Firestore Security Rules — users can only access their own data; self-like prevention enforced at rules level
  • Chat message delivery rate limiting (daily limit for free users)
  • Blocked users stored locally only — block relationships not exposed to third parties
  • No crash reporting SDK — errors handled gracefully in-app without external transmission
  • Offline error suppression — network failures never cause error screens or data loss
  • Regular security reviews and dependency updates

⏰ USE_FULL_SCREEN_INTENT — Why We Use It

This permission is used exclusively for the habit alarm system. When a scheduled habit alarm fires, HabitNode displays a full-screen alert that:

  • Activates your screen and bypasses the lock screen
  • Plays an alarm sound and haptic feedback
  • Announces the habit name via TTS (Text-to-Speech)
  • Offers Snooze (5 minutes) and Dismiss options

The lock screen bypass is active only during the alarm and is disabled immediately after dismissal or snooze. This is a core habit-reminder feature and poses no ongoing security risk.
