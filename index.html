1. Information We Collect

1.1 Data Stored Locally on Your Device

The following data is stored exclusively on your device using the Hive encrypted local database and is never transmitted to our servers unless you explicitly enable a cloud feature:

  • Profile Information: Display name, avatar emoji, tagline, bio, country code (all optional)
  • Habit & Routine Data: Habit names, descriptions, categories, schedules, goals, priorities, notes, completion records, study targets, and progress logs
  • Study Sessions: Pomodoro timer data, study routine schedules, session history, subject targets, and daily study time records
  • Personal Notes: Note titles, content, tags, and timestamps
  • Missed Habit Reflections: Optional reasons provided when skipping a habit — used locally for smart reminders only
  • Achievement Data: Badges earned, XP points, level progress, streaks, and trophy cabinet
  • App Preferences: Theme mode, notification settings, sound settings, alarm configurations, and privacy toggles
  • Alarm Configurations: Alarm times, custom alarm sounds, snooze preferences, vibration settings — all stored locally and never transmitted
  • Study Analytics: Daily, weekly, and all-time study minutes — stored locally for statistics display
  • Blocked Users List: UIDs of users you have blocked — stored locally only, never sent to any server
  • Liked Profiles Cache: Local reference to profiles you have liked — used to display your "You Liked" tab on the Leaderboard screen without repeated network calls

1.2 Data Stored in the Cloud (Optional & User-Initiated)

📊 Global Leaderboard & Public Profile (Opt-In Required) v1.0.5

If you voluntarily join the global leaderboard, the following data is stored in Google Cloud Firestore:

  • Display name, avatar emoji, tagline, bio, country code (all editable by you)
  • Unique UID — your Firebase Authentication UID
  • XP score, level, study hours, badges unlocked count
  • Daily score, weekly score
  • Posts you share on the Social Feed
  • Privacy settings: showLevel, showBadges, showStudyHours toggles
  • Pro status (boolean — not payment details)
  • Last active timestamp
  • Like count: Total number of likes received on your profile (integer only — not individual liker identities in the main document)

Your Privacy Controls: You may toggle visibility of your level, badges, and study hours independently. You may opt out or permanently delete your cloud profile at any time via Settings → Leaderboard → Opt Out or Delete Profile.

The Leaderboard screen includes three tabs: Weekly, All Time, and You Liked (showing profiles you have liked).

⚠️ What other users can see: Only your display name, avatar, tagline, bio, country code, XP, level (if enabled), badges (if enabled), study time (if enabled), social posts, and total like count. Your email address is never shown to other users. Use of leaderboard features is governed by our Terms & Conditions.

❤️ Profile Like System (Opt-In — Leaderboard Required) v1.0.5

HabitNode includes a Profile Like feature that allows users to show appreciation for other users' public profiles. When you use this feature:

  • What is stored in Firestore: When you like a profile, your UID and a server-generated timestamp are stored in a likes subcollection under that user's Firestore document
  • What the profile owner sees: Total like count on their profile, and a list of users who liked them (showing display name and avatar, fetched from their public profile)
  • What other users see: Only the total like count — not who liked the profile
  • "You Liked" Tab: On the Leaderboard screen (alongside Weekly and All Time tabs), you can view all profiles you have liked. This list is fetched from Firestore and cached locally for performance
  • Unlike: You may remove your like at any time by tapping the like button again — your UID and timestamp are immediately and permanently deleted from Firestore
  • Self-like prevention: You cannot like your own profile — enforced both in-app and at the Firestore rules level

Data stored per like record in Firestore:

  • likerUid — your Firebase UID
  • likedAt — server timestamp of when the like was created

Deletion: If you delete your cloud profile, all like records associated with your UID as the liker are removed. If the liked profile is deleted, the entire likes subcollection is removed automatically.

🔒 Like data is never shared with advertisers, third parties, or used for profiling purposes. It exists solely to enable the social appreciation feature within HabitNode.

☁️ Google Drive Auto-Backup (User-Initiated)

When you enable Google Drive backup, your data is encrypted and uploaded to your private Google Drive appDataFolder. This folder is invisible to other apps and accessible only by you and HabitNode.

Data covered by backup: All habits, routines, study sessions, study targets, leaderboard profile (local copy), personal notes, app settings, preferences, alarm configurations, and notification history.

Auto-Restore on Sign-In: When you sign in with Google on a new device or after reinstalling, HabitNode automatically checks for an existing Google Drive backup and restores your data silently in the background.

Backup Frequency Options: Every change (with debounce), daily, weekly, or on app exit. Works on both WiFi and mobile data. An offline pending queue ensures no data loss when connectivity is unavailable.

Chat messages and profile likes are NOT included in Google Drive backup — they are stored in Firestore and managed separately.

💬 Social Chat & Direct Messaging (Optional — Account Required)

HabitNode includes a built-in real-time direct messaging system. Messages are transmitted over HTTPS/TLS encrypted channels and stored in Google Cloud Firestore. Free users have a daily message limit (20 messages/day); Pro users have unlimited messaging.

Your controls: Delete individual messages (Unsend) · Clear entire chat history · Block users · Report users

🔒 Chat usage is subject to our Terms & Conditions.

📢 Social Feed & Posts (Optional)

If you choose to share a post on the Social Feed (maximum 500 words per post), your author UID, display name, avatar emoji, XP score, post text, and timestamps are stored publicly in Firestore.

2. Alarm & Lock Screen Features v1.0.5

🔔 Professional Lock-Screen Alarm System

HabitNode includes a professional-grade alarm system designed to reliably wake you up and remind you of your habits, similar to standard alarm clock apps. This section explains how it works and what data it handles.

2.1 What the Alarm System Does

  • Lock Screen Display: When an alarm fires, HabitNode displays a full-screen alarm interface directly over your lock screen — no unlock required
  • Screen Wake-Up: The alarm automatically turns on your device screen, even if it was off
  • Loud Sound Playback: Alarm sounds play through the alarm audio stream (not media), bypassing silent mode when applicable
  • Vibration: Device vibrates during alarm (if vibration is enabled in settings)
  • Snooze & Dismiss: Two action buttons — "Stop Alarm" (marks habit as done) and "Snooze 5 minutes"
  • Background Reliability: Alarms fire even when the app is closed, the device is locked, or in battery-saving mode
  • Boot Persistence: Alarms automatically re-schedule themselves after device reboot

2.2 Security Lockdown During Alarm Important

⚠️ Alarm Screen Lockdown: When an alarm is actively ringing on your locked device, the alarm interface is the only accessible screen. This is an intentional security design to prevent unauthorized access to your app data while your device remains locked.

What this means:

  • The Back button is disabled during alarm
  • Home gesture/button cannot dismiss the alarm — only the in-app Stop or Snooze buttons can
  • System UI (status bar, navigation) is hidden during alarm
  • If you attempt to exit, the alarm screen automatically reappears
  • Screenshots and screen recording are disabled on the alarm screen (FLAG_SECURE)
  • Once you press Stop Alarm or Snooze, you are returned to your device's lock screen (the app remains locked behind it)

🔒 This ensures that even if someone steals your device while an alarm is ringing, they cannot access your habit data, notes, or any other content.

2.3 What Data the Alarm System Collects

✅ Zero Data Collection from Alarms

The alarm system operates 100% locally on your device. We collect no data from alarm operations:

  • ❌ No audio is recorded
  • ❌ No microphone is accessed
  • ❌ Snooze/dismiss actions are not tracked on our servers
  • ❌ Alarm trigger times are not sent to any server
  • ❌ No usage statistics are gathered
  • ✅ Alarm configurations are stored only on your device (included in optional Google Drive backup if enabled)

2.4 Background Service for Alarms

To ensure reliable alarm sound playback, HabitNode runs a brief foreground service when an alarm fires. This service:

  • Starts only when an alarm is triggered at the user-scheduled time
  • Plays the alarm sound through the device's alarm audio stream
  • Stops automatically when you press Stop or Snooze
  • Does not collect, transmit, or store any data
  • Does not run continuously in the background

2.5 User Control Over Alarms

You have full control over the alarm system. You can:

  • Disable per-habit: Turn off alarms for individual habits in habit settings
  • Disable globally: Turn off all alarms via App Settings → Notifications
  • Revoke system permissions: Go to Android Settings → Apps → HabitNode → Permissions → Disable "Alarms & Reminders" and "Display over other apps"
  • Customize sounds: Use default alarm sound or select a custom sound from your device
  • Customize snooze duration: Default is 5 minutes (adjustable in settings)

2.6 Alarm Liability Disclaimer

⚠️ Not for Critical/Safety Use: While HabitNode employs industry-standard mechanisms (full-screen intents, exact alarms, wake locks, foreground service) for reliable alarm delivery, alarm functionality may be affected by:

  • Device-specific battery optimization (especially on Xiaomi/MIUI, Huawei/EMUI, Samsung/OneUI, Oppo, Vivo, Realme)
  • Manufacturer-customized power management
  • Do Not Disturb mode
  • Airplane mode or device shutdown
  • Operating system updates or restrictions
  • Insufficient device storage or RAM

You acknowledge that HabitNode alarms should not be solely relied upon for critical, life-safety, medical, or emergency purposes. For guaranteed alarm delivery, we recommend whitelisting HabitNode from battery optimization in your device settings. VHIR Tech is not liable for missed alarms or any consequences thereof.

3. Connectivity-Based Behavior

📵 Offline-First Architecture

HabitNode is designed to work fully offline. All habit tracking, study timer, notes, alarms, notifications, and local analytics work without any network connection. Cloud features are gracefully disabled when offline — no data loss occurs. Cached leaderboard data and "You Liked" tab profiles display from local cache. When connectivity returns, cloud operations resume automatically.
