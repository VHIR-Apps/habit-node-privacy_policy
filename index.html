Privacy Policy

HabitNode — Habit Tracker App

Last Updated: January 19, 2025

This Privacy Policy describes how HabitNode ("the App", "we", "us", "our"), developed by VHIR, collects, uses, and protects your information when you use our mobile application available on the Google Play Store.

By downloading, installing, or using HabitNode, you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

🔐 Privacy-First Design: HabitNode stores most of your data locally on your device. Cloud features (Leaderboard, Google Drive Backup) are OPTIONAL and require your explicit consent.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide (Stored Locally)

When using HabitNode, you may provide the following information, which is stored locally on your device only:

  • Profile Information: Display name and avatar emoji (optional, stored locally)
  • Habit Data: Habit names, descriptions, categories, schedules, goals, priorities, notes, and completion records
  • Study Sessions & Routines: Pomodoro timer data, study routine schedules, session history
  • Notes: Personal notes with title, content, tags, and timestamps
  • Missed Habit Reasons: When you miss a habit, you may optionally provide a reason (stored locally for smart reminders)
  • Achievement Data: Badges earned, XP points, level progress, streaks
  • App Preferences: Theme mode, notification settings, sound settings, alarm configurations

1.2 Information Stored in Cloud (Optional, User-Initiated)

The following features are 100% OPTIONAL and require your explicit action:

📊 Leaderboard Feature (Optional, Opt-In)

If you choose to join the global leaderboard by tapping "Join Leaderboard" in Settings, the following data is stored in Google Cloud Firestore:

  • Display Name: Your chosen username (visible to other users)
  • Avatar Emoji: Your profile emoji (visible to other users)
  • Total Score: Your total XP points
  • Level: Your current level
  • Total Habits: Number of habits you've created
  • Account UID: Your Firebase Authentication user ID (not visible to others)
  • Country Code: Your country (if available from Google Sign-In)
  • Opt-In Status: Whether you're currently participating in the leaderboard
  • Timestamps: When you joined and last updated your profile
✅ You Control Leaderboard Data: You can opt-out or delete your cloud profile anytime from Settings → Leaderboard → Opt Out or Delete Profile.

☁️ Google Drive Backup (Optional, User-Initiated)

If you choose to enable Google Drive backup in Settings, your habit data (habits, routines, notes, settings) is encrypted and uploaded to your private Google Drive app folder. Only you can access this data. We do not have access to your Google Drive files.

1.3 Information Collected Automatically

The App itself does NOT collect any personal information automatically. However, the following third-party services integrated in the App may collect certain data:

1.4 Information We Do NOT Collect

We do NOT collect:

  • Your phone number or physical address
  • Precise location data or GPS coordinates
  • Camera or microphone data
  • Contacts, call logs, or SMS messages
  • Browsing history or search queries
  • Biometric data (fingerprints, face recognition)
  • Financial information or payment card details (handled entirely by Google Play)
  • Health or fitness data from device sensors
  • Device IMEI or serial numbers

2. How We Use Information

2.1 Local Data Usage (No Internet Required)

Data stored locally on your device is used for:

  • Habit Tracking: To display, manage, and track your daily habits and routines
  • Notifications & Alarms: To send you scheduled reminders and full-screen alarms for your habits
  • Smart Reminders: To generate personalized reminder messages based on your missed habit reasons (processed locally, never sent to any server)
  • Statistics: To calculate and display your completion rates, streaks, and progress charts
  • Badge & XP System: To track your achievements, award badges, calculate experience points, and determine your level
  • Study Mode: To track Pomodoro timer sessions, study routines, and productivity analytics
  • Notes: To create, edit, search, and organize your personal notes
  • Text-to-Speech: To speak habit names and alarm descriptions aloud using your device's built-in TTS engine
  • Sound Effects: To play audio feedback for habit completions, badge unlocks, and other app interactions

2.2 Cloud Data Usage (Optional Features Only)

If you opt-in to cloud features:

  • Leaderboard: Your username, avatar, and score are displayed in the global leaderboard ranking. Other users can see your public profile (username, avatar, level, score) but NOT your habits or personal data.
  • Google Drive Backup: Your encrypted backup file is uploaded to your private Google Drive folder for restore purposes. We cannot access your Google Drive data.

2.3 Third-Party Service Usage

  • Advertising: To display relevant advertisements (free users only) through Google AdMob and Unity Ads
  • In-App Purchases: To process Pro subscription purchases through Google Play Billing
  • Authentication: To securely sign you in with Google for leaderboard access (optional)
💡 Smart Reminders: When you provide a reason for missing a habit, the app generates a personalized notification message locally on your device using simple text templates. This feature does NOT use AI, machine learning, or send your data to any external server.

3. Data Storage & Security

3.1 Local Storage (Primary Data Storage)

All core app data is stored locally on your device using Hive, a lightweight, secure local database. Data is organized in separate storage containers (boxes):

  • Habits Box: All habit data, completion records, missed reasons, and progress
  • Study Sessions Box: Pomodoro timer data, study routines, session history
  • Notes Box: All your personal notes with metadata
  • Settings Box: App preferences, theme, notification settings, purchase status
  • Badges Box: Achievement data, XP points, badge unlock records, usage statistics

3.2 Cloud Storage (Optional, User-Controlled)

Google Cloud Firestore (Leaderboard Only):

  • Used ONLY if you opt-in to the leaderboard feature
  • Stores only your public profile data (username, avatar, score, level, country)
  • Secured with Firestore Security Rules that ensure you can only modify your own profile
  • Indexed for efficient leaderboard queries (ordered by score and update time)
  • You can delete your cloud profile anytime from Settings

Google Drive (Backup Only):

  • Used ONLY if you manually initiate a backup from Settings
  • Files are stored in your private app folder (appDataFolder scope)
  • We do NOT have access to your Google Drive files or other app data
  • You control backup and restore through Google Drive settings

3.3 Data Security Measures

We implement industry-standard security practices:

  • Local Encryption: Sensitive data is encrypted using Hive's built-in encryption
  • Secure Authentication: Firebase Authentication with Google OAuth 2.0
  • Firestore Rules: User data is protected by server-side security rules
  • HTTPS Only: All network requests use encrypted HTTPS connections
  • No Plain Text Passwords: We never store or transmit passwords (Google handles authentication)

Your data is as secure as your device itself. We recommend:

  • Using a screen lock (PIN, pattern, fingerprint, or face unlock) on your device
  • Keeping your device's operating system updated
  • Not rooting or jailbreaking your device
  • Regularly backing up your habit data using the in-app backup feature

3.4 Backup Files

When you use the local backup feature, your data is exported as a JSON file to your device's storage. When you use Google Drive backup, an encrypted backup is uploaded to your private Google Drive folder. You are solely responsible for the security of exported backup files.

14. Security Measures

We take data security seriously and implement the following measures:

14.1 Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption in Transit: All network requests use HTTPS/TLS encryption
  • Encryption at Rest: Sensitive local data is encrypted using Hive encryption
  • Secure Authentication: Firebase Authentication with Google OAuth 2.0 (no password storage)
  • Firestore Security Rules: Server-side rules prevent unauthorized access to other users' data
  • Input Validation: All user inputs are sanitized to prevent injection attacks
  • No Hardcoded Secrets: API keys and credentials are stored securely and obfuscated

14.2 Organizational Safeguards

  • Access to cloud data (Firestore) is restricted to authorized developers only
